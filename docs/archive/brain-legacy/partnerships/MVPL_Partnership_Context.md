## Background — How We Got Here

### The Original Commercial Relationship
ARUSHAI approached TI Industries (now MVPL) as a software
vendor. Proposed Feed Mill Intelligence (FMI) module with
three pricing options:
- Option A: ₹3L implementation + ₹60K/month subscription
- Option B: ₹4.5L implementation + ₹75K/month subscription
- Option C: ₹6L implementation + ₹1L/month subscription

PPT presentation delivered. Pricing shared. MD engaged
deeply — asked technical questions about PLC integration,
MIS, lab testing AI, timeline.

### Why Subscription Pricing Failed
MVPL MD rejected the subscription model. Core reason:
poultry business has unpredictable quarterly losses.
Egg and chicken market prices are volatile. Some quarters
MVPL runs at a loss. Fixed monthly fees are not viable
when revenue itself is unpredictable.

This is a legitimate objection — not a negotiation tactic.
The business genuinely cannot commit to fixed fees
during bad quarters.

### The Partnership Offer
Instead of a vendor relationship, MVPL MD proposed:
Irfan joins MVPL as Technology Partner. Takes charge of
all technology decisions, development, and advancement
across the entire company. Remote from Qatar. Equity-based
compensation rather than monthly fees.

### Current Status
3-4 day thinking period. Irfan asked MD to also think and
propose what equity structure he has in mind. Next
conversation pending. Neither side has committed to anything.

## The Core Strategic Questions

These are the questions this session must work through
systematically. Do not rush any of them.

### Question 1 — Is this the right opportunity?

Sub-questions:
- Is MVPL's IPO vision realistic or aspirational?
- Is the MD the kind of partner who will respect
  technology decisions even when he disagrees?
- Is the business financially stable enough to survive
  the 3-5 year build period without hitting existential
  cash flow problems?
- What happens to Irfan's equity if MVPL hits a
  bad year and needs to raise emergency capital?

### Question 2 — What structure protects ARUSHAI?

The non-negotiable: ARUSHAI must survive as an
independent entity. The platform Irfan builds must
be licensable to other clients. IP must stay with ARUSHAI.

Proposed structure to explore:
- MVPL receives X% equity in ARUSHAI
  (founding partner + operational laboratory)
- Irfan receives Y% equity in MVPL
  (technology co-founder)
- ARUSHAI retains all IP
- ARUSHAI licenses platform to MVPL
- MVPL pays ARUSHAI a monthly retainer
  (covers build costs — not profit)
- ARUSHAI can serve other clients

Questions to refine:
- What is the right X and Y?
- What does "exclusive" mean — can ARUSHAI serve
  other poultry companies in Karnataka while MVPL
  is a client? Other states?
- What is the retainer amount that makes this
  financially viable for Irfan?

### Question 3 — What does IP ownership look like in practice?

Everything Irfan builds: the code, the AI models,
the platform architecture, the trained intelligence
from MVPL's operational data.

Key distinctions:
- MVPL's operational data stays private to MVPL
- AI models trained on that data — who owns the model?
- Platform architecture — ARUSHAI owns
- Custom configurations for MVPL — who owns?
- If partnership ends — who can use what?

### Question 4 — What is the financial bridge?

Equity in an IPO-track company is worth nothing today.
Irfan has monthly expenses in Qatar.

Options to explore:
- Monthly retainer from MVPL to ARUSHAI
  (ARUSHAI pays Irfan from this)
- Project-based fees for specific deliverables
- Salary as MVPL's technology head
  (simplest but creates employee relationship, not partner)
- Combination: smaller retainer + equity stake

What is the minimum monthly that makes this viable?
What is the number that makes it genuinely attractive?

### Question 5 — Who handles IoT hardware on the ground?

Irfan is in Qatar. Installing and maintaining temperature
sensors at 470+ farms across Karnataka is not remote work.

Options:
- MVPL hires a hardware operations team (their cost)
- ARUSHAI partners with an IoT hardware company
  (revenue share model)
- Hardware is Phase 2 — software first, hardware later
  once relationship is proven

This cannot be left vague. Ground-level hardware
requires human presence that Irfan cannot provide.

### Question 6 — What are the personal non-negotiables?

Before any business structure conversation:
- Family must be informed and supportive
- Qatar life and stability must be protected
- Physical presence in Karnataka cannot be a requirement
- Mental bandwidth limits must be honest
  (a day job + ARUSHAI + MVPL partnership = 3 things)

Is the day job compatible with this partnership?
At what point does the partnership replace the day job?
What financial milestone triggers that transition?

- MD engages seriously with IP conversation
- Willingness to involve a lawyer from both sides
- Proposes equity structure before being asked
- Open to ARUSHAI serving other clients
- Respects technology decisions even without understanding them
- Prepared to commit financial compensation during build phase

## Competitive Landscape — What MVPL Is Up Against
(And Why ARUSHAI's Partnership Value Is Larger Than It Appears)

### The Tulasi Reality Check

MVPL MD referenced Tulasi Technologies as the benchmark
company they want to surpass. After research, here is the
complete honest picture.

**What Tulasi actually has:**
18 years old. Bangalore. 11-50 employees. Clients across
India, Middle East, Africa, APAC. GPS supervisor tracking,
Bluetooth weighing scales, WhatsApp integration, IoT feed
silo monitoring, camera systems, full feed mill module.

**How Tulasi markets itself:**
They publish domain knowledge frameworks to attract clients.
Recent example — "Broiler Gut Health Stability Framework":
a Wednesday manual checklist with 5 health indicators
supervisors should look for in the Day 12-28 critical window.
Feed intake drop >4%. Water:feed ratio above 2.4.
Wet droppings. Body weight uniformity below 82%.
Undigested feed in litter. Their financial hook:
0.07 FCR drift = significant per-cycle margin loss.

**The critical gap this reveals:**
Tulasi is telling supervisors what to look for manually.
A human visits one farm on Wednesday. Observes. Reports back.

ARUSHAI automates all 5 indicators across all 470 farms
simultaneously in real time. Not on Wednesday.
Every hour. Every farm. With automated alerts.

**This is ARUSHAI's permanent competitive position:**
Every protocol Tulasi publishes as a manual checklist
becomes an ARUSHAI automated agent. They are 18 years old
and still building software that records what happened.
ARUSHAI builds intelligence that predicts what will happen.

**Why this matters for the partnership conversation:**
When you negotiate equity with the MD — you are not
negotiating for a software tool. You are negotiating for
the technology that makes MVPL the most intelligent
poultry integrator in India — permanently ahead of Tulasi
in ways Tulasi cannot replicate without rebuilding from scratch.
That is a co-founder level contribution. Not a vendor fee.
